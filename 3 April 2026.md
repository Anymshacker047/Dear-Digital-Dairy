# Dear-Digital-Dairy-3-April-2026-
# Digital Diary: Journey to Becoming a Digital Forensics Analyst

**Date:** 11 January – April

---

## Entry 1: The Beginning
It all started around **11 January**, when I filled out a government form for the Cyber Volutior program. My dream was clear — I wanted to become a **Digital Forensics Analyst and Investigator**, working at the Police Headquarters and solving real cases.

> *“कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन। मा कर्मफलहेतुर्भूर्मा ते सङ्गोऽस्त्वकर्मणि॥”* — भगवत गीता 2.47  
> *(Your work is to perform your duty, but never be attached to the results.)*

The reply came on **28 March**, right when I was handling a fraud case for a company. Soon after, I was added to a WhatsApp group, and by **2 April**, I received a message about my interview — **scheduled for 3 April at 2 PM**.

---

## Entry 2: The Challenge Ahead
I was shocked 😳. My home was **350 km away**, and even by train, reaching on time was tough. My mom insisted, *“You have to try your best.”* I had **no CV** and no idea what the interview would involve.

> *“मनुष्य अपने लक्ष्य तक तब तक शांत नहीं होता जब तक वह उसकी तैयारी न कर ले। तैयारी करो और ब्रह्मांड अपने दरवाजे खोल देगा।”*  
> *(Man is restless until it finds its purpose. Prepare yourself and the universe will open doors.)*

---

## Entry 3: Guidance and Preparation
By chance, my **Digital Forensics teacher’s institute was only half an hour away**. He guided me and **helped me prepare a full CV**, highlighting:  

- **4 years of practical experience solving real cases**  
- Skills in investigation, case handling, and interacting with people  
- Hands-on knowledge from real-life scenarios  

> *“योगस्थः कुरु कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा धनञ्जय। सिद्ध्यसिद्ध्योः समो भूत्वा समत्वं योग उच्यते॥”* — भगवत गीता 2.48  
> *(Do your work with full dedication, letting go of attachment to results; equanimity in success and failure is called yoga.)*

Honestly, the CV was so detailed, my teacher practically **filled every page** 😂.

---

## Entry 4: The Interview Day
With **2 hours to go**, I was tense. The good thing? My teacher’s office and the Police Headquarters were **only 10 minutes apart**.

- My teacher spent **1.5 hours boosting my confidence**  
- Gave me a **practice interview**, walked me through the process  
- I finally reached the Police Headquarters, still trembling  

> *“योगः कर्मसु कौशलम्।”* — भगवत गीता 2.50  
> *(Skill in action is yoga; performing your duties efficiently is the path to success.)*

---

## Entry 5: First Impressions
Upon entering, I was directed straight to the **Rajasthan Head of Cyber Crime’s room**. At first glance, he seemed intimidating, but I realized he was **humble and dedicated, solving a case even while meeting me**.

- I felt my **8 years of preparation** finally paying off  
- The conversation started with **small questions**, then gradually shifted to **advanced discussions**  
- For **1 hour**, it felt like a normal case discussion, as if I was just talking to peers  

> *“सर्वधर्मान्परित्यज्य मामेकं शरणं व्रज। अहं त्वां सर्वपापेभ्यो मोक्षयिष्यामि मा शुचः॥”* — भगवत गीता 18.66  
> *(Abandon all duties that conflict with your higher purpose and surrender to me; I will liberate you from all sins.)*

---

## Entry 6: The Thrill of Recognition
Suddenly, he said:  
> *“Go explore, observe… this is your work now. This is where you belong.”*

- My jaw dropped 😲  
- **Excitement overflowed** — the dream I had since 6th class was finally coming true  
- I realized: **years of preparation, patience, and dedication had led me here**  

> *“कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन।”* — भगवत गीता 2.47  
> *(Do your duty without attachment to the result; focus on action, not outcome.)*

---

## Entry 7: Celebration and Human Joy
After a bit of **paperwork**, I wandered Jaipur a little, enjoying **Ghawer** — delicious local treat 😋  
I called home after reaching, and **everyone was happy for me**. Even after **4 hours of travel**, the party and celebration continued.

- Despite the cold air giving me a slight **cold**, the **joy and excitement** couldn’t be stopped  
- Police officers congratulated me, and the feeling was **unreal**  
- Years of struggle, preparation, and nervousness transformed into **pure motivation and pride**  

> *“योगस्थः कुरु कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा धनञ्जय।”* — भगवत गीता 2.48  
> *(Be steady in action, balanced in success and failure; this is yoga.)*

---

## Reflections
This journey taught me:  

- **Preparation beats fear**  
- **Humility and human connection** matter more than attitude  
- **Dreams can become reality**, no matter how long the journey has been  
- **Celebration after struggle is essential**, even amidst small challenges  

> *“सुखदुःखे समे कृत्वा लाभालाभौ जयाजयौ। ततो युद्धाय युज्यस्व नैवं पापमवाप्स्यसि॥”* — भगवत गीता 2.38  
> *(Be balanced in happiness and distress, gain and loss, victory and defeat. Engage in action without sin.)*

---

### **End of Entry**
This diary reflects **years of struggle, learning, and realization**, ending with both professional achievement and personal joy.
